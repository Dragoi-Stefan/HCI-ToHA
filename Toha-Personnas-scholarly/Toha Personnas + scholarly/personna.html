Type: Primary

Name: Emily Thompson

Background
  • Date of Birth: 10/23/1990
  • Gender: Female
  • Location: London, UK
  • Work place: Tour Guide
Main Points
She is an experienced tour guide, tech-savvy and open to using digital tools, responsible for managing itinerary and coordinating with group members.
Goals

Goals are the reasons users perform tasks, not the tasks.

  • Practical Goals: Coordinate with group members and provide them with updated schedules and information;Enable seamless communication among group members for real-time updates and coordination.
  • Personal Goals: Provide a sense of community and engagement among group members through group and individual chats;Facilitate a memorable and enjoyable trip by offering timers and alarms for each location.
  • Business Goals: Establish a reputation for excellent tour management and customer satisfaction;Increase customer loyalty and repeat business by delivering a seamless and well-organized trip experience.
Frustrations and Pain Points

Some of the difficulties the user has with the product.

  • Coordinating with group members in real-time: The Tourist Group Leader may face challenges in coordinating with group members on the go, especially when changes or updates need to be communicated quickly. This can lead to delays, miscommunication, and confusion within the group.
  • Managing last-minute changes to the itinerary: Dealing with unexpected changes, such as weather conditions or unforeseen circumstances, can be stressful for the Tourist Group Leader. Adjusting schedules, rearranging activities, and ensuring everyone is informed and on the same page can be challenging.
  • Ensuring everyone has access to updated information: Keeping everyone in the group up to date with the latest schedules, directions, and information about points of interest can be difficult. Ensuring that all group members have access to and receive the updated information in a timely manner can be a pain point.
Detailed Description

Emily is an experienced tour guide who is responsible for leading a group of tourists on a trip. She needs a user-friendly interface that allows her to easily manage the itinerary, communicate with group members, and provide updated information.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: Notifying the group about a change in the schedule due to weather conditions.

Emily, the Tourist Group Leader, receives information about an upcoming storm that will affect the planned outdoor activity. She needs to quickly notify all the group members about the change in the schedule to ensure their safety and make alternative arrangements. Emily opens the mobile application, navigates to the group chat feature, and sends a message to all the group members, informing them about the change in the itinerary and providing the new activity details and meeting location. The group members receive the notification on their devices, acknowledging the change and confirming their understanding.

Scenario 2: Facilitating individual chats to address specific questions or concerns.

Emily, the Tourist Group Leader, notices that some group members have individual questions or concerns regarding the upcoming visit to a historical site. She opens the mobile application and accesses the individual chat feature, which allows her to have private conversations with each group member. Emily initiates individual chats with the concerned group members to address their specific questions, provide additional information, or offer reassurance. She engages in personalized conversations, addressing their concerns and providing guidance on specific aspects of the visit, such as accessibility, parking, or recommended time slots. Emily ensures that all group members feel heard, supported, and well-informed, fostering a sense of confidence and anticipation for the upcoming visit. The group members appreciate the opportunity to have their individual questions addressed, enhancing their overall experience and easing any uncertainties they may have had.

Interaction types/paradigms

Traditional versus gestural+haptic

In the context of our app design, we have chosen to primarily utilize traditional interaction methods, such as buttons with describable icons, for specific operations. This approach provides users with clear visual cues and allows them to initiate actions by tapping on the relevant buttons.
But we also have gestural interactions that involve using touch gestures, such as swiping, mainly on the popular location areas where we have a scrollable to the right view. The cut image suggests the scrollable view.

Mixed Reality Interaction

By utilizing the camera functionality of the app to overlay digital information and descriptions on top of real-world POIs, we are creating a mixed reality experience for users. This feature allows users like Sarah and John to visually perceive their surroundings while receiving additional contextual information and guidance through the app.

Conversational User Interface (CUI) versus Classical I/O

We maintain classic inputs with a simple design. A good idea for conversational UI would be on the search bar.
