1. Abstract

This scolarly HTML document presents an analysis of the design and user experience aspects of a mobile and smart watch app developed for organizing trips for tourist groups. The app incorporates features such as augmented reality for points of interest and also navigation. The document will explore interaction design patterns, user flows, affective factors and emotions within the app and also story boards diagrams with in depth explanations.

2. Introduction

To the most of us, travel and exploration have become integral parts of our lives. For the needs of travelers, we have developed the design for a mobile app that focuses on simplifying the organization for groups of tourists, or for solo travelers, as the app contains a lot of features that do not require a group. These features are organized in a user-friendly interface, that emphasize these innovative functionalities that include augmented reality (AR) and point of interest navigation based on user photos of current location.

The motivation behind the development of this app is the desire to adress the challenges faced by travelers when organizing trips for groups. Existing solutions fail to provide an intuitive and egaging user experience. Our app aims to narrow this gap by incorporating techonologies and effective interaction design patterns. Some challenges faced by travelers are the possibilities of getting lost in specific areas where some gps app does not give exact directions or it is hard to understand where to move. For this specific problem there comes the AR feature incorporated in our app.

By examining these key areas and pain points of the users, we aim to contribute valuable insights to the field of mobile travel applications.

3. Interaction Design Patterns

3.1 Scrollable Sections

The home page of our app features three sections, two of them which are scrollable horizontally, each presenting different sets of information (popular locations near the user's area, popular destinations and a section that gives access to the tools of the app). This pattern allows users to navigate horizontally to access more content, providing a visually engaging and efficient way to explore different categories of information.

3.2 Augmented Reality (AR)

The AR feature enables uses to capture photos of points of interest, this resulting in the display of relevant details about that POI. Also, this feature also allow the user to use the camera to highligh his current location and on the phone and then suggestions for the tracking of the desired point of interest will appear, like arrows and distance remaining before the next turn.This pattern leverages the capabilities of mobile devices to overlay digital information onto the real-world view, enhancing the user's understanding and interaction with the physical environment.

3.3 Mobile specific UI Patterns

Navigation

For navigation, we thought that a bottom navigation menu bar would suit the app the best. The bottom navigation has suggestive icons for each of the desired action that is incorporated in the app, also, this type of navigation is best suited for mobile apps because of the small distance of the thumb to the actual button that the user wants to press.

Layout

For layout mostly we chose cards and grids. We chose cards to represent the locations we present in the home page. Each card has information about the place with a relevant image and a rating provided by Google.

Form patterns

Regarding forms we use a placeholder for the search functionality of our app, located on the home page, to suggest users to click on it and search for the desired visiting place. When a place is selected a multi step form will be triggered, that basically creates the experience and the scedule for the group that the specific user wants. In this form we used toggles, calendar picker, time picker and a text input for the name of the activity.

4. User Flows

Registration and Group Creation: Users would need to register or log in to the app, after which they can create a group and invite friends to join.

Trip Organization: Once a group is created, the user can organize a trip to a specific location, including selecting dates, setting preferences, and making necessary arrangements.

Point of View and Schedule Management: Users can then create a schedule for visiting specific points of view or cities, assigning time slots, adding descriptions, and managing the itinerary for the trip.

5. Affective Factors and Emotion

5.1 Delight

The app's interface and features should aim to evoke positive emotions and a sense of excitement as users discover popular locations, explore destinations, and interact with augmented reality to gain information and directions.

5.2 Accomplishment

Completing trip schedules, successfully navigating to POIs, or receiving positive feedback from group members give users a sense of achievement and progress.
